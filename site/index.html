The 4 PARTS OF MEDICARE Simplified...

Overview

There are four parts to Medicare. Parts A, B, C or a supplement and D. Parts A and B are provided to you by the United States government (Social Security) when you turn 65 or become eligible for disability. Parts C, and Part D is provided to you by a private insurance company.

PART A | Hospital Insurance

Insurance to help pay for your hospital bills. Covers about 80% of your hospital bills.

PART B | Medical Insurance

Insurance for Doctor visits, surgeries, x-rays, and outpatient services. Covers 80%.

PART C | Advantage or Supplement

Insurance that fills in the gaps of Original Medicare. Covers the remaining 20%.

PART D | Prescription Drugs

Insurance to help pay for your prescription drugs. Copays are based on your individual medications.
